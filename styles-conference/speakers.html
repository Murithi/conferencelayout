Topic 2018

The youth glimpses the world in its greatness and imperfections. In the university, a student encounters both wonder and dissatisfaction. One feels a desire for change, along with a certain insecurity one never knows whether one’s efforts will be successful. It is now that the university student confronts himself with the hardest questions to answer: Where do I come from? Where am I going? What do I want to do? The answers mean everything. When you know who you are and what role you play in the world, your perspectives and horizons open up, and you can propose changes in society to make it better. 50 years ago, this same restlessness led to a revolution that overthrew all points of reference. Freedom was confused with unruliness, opening the way to the current liquid society. Like all ideologies, the student revolution of May 1968 did not do justice to the authentic greatness of the human person. To be effective, every paradigm shift needs reflection; it needs to listen to the deepest truth of the human heart. Otherwise, the “revolution” ends in chaos and debris. Are you a rebel, a non-conformist, a hero? It is there, in the university–with your friends, in the street–where you discover your professional vocation, your social vocation, your family vocation, your divine vocation and you will discover that, from that place, you can become a protagonist of history.

We’re happy to welcome over twenty fantastic speakers to present on the intellectual wealth of our faith. Prepare for a profound experience, an inspiring extravaganza.
